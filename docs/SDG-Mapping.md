# Salma Unity Care Hospital — SDG & Impact Mapping

This document maps Salma Unity Care Hospital modules to the UN Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs), showing how the hospital’s blockchain-integrated services produce measurable civic impact.

---

## SDG summary table

| SDG | Focus | Hospital module alignment | Primary impact |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| SDG 3 | Good Health & Well‑being | Patient identity, encrypted records, clinical workflows | Safer care, continuity, outcomes |
| SDG 9 | Industry, Innovation & Infrastructure | Healthcare data rails, payments, APIs | Resilient digital health infra |

---

## SDG 3 — Good health and well‑being

- **Patient identity & access:** Verifiable credentials for patients, clinicians, and staff; role‑based access to records improves safety and reduces errors.
- **Encrypted medical records:** End‑to‑end encryption, consent logs, and immutable audit trails strengthen continuity of care and data protection.
- **Clinical workflows:** Time‑stamped care pathways, lab/radiology attachments, and discharge summaries improve quality outcomes.

---

## SDG 9 — Industry, innovation and infrastructure

- **Health data rails:** Standardized data models and APIs support interoperability across hospitals, insurers, and public health systems.
- **Payments infrastructure:** ISO 20022‑aligned rails for billing, claims, and settlements reduce friction and delays.
- **High availability:** Redundant nodes, secure key management, and monitored uptime create resilient healthcare infrastructure.

---

## SDG 16 — Peace, justice and strong institutions

- **Consent governance:** Explicit consent capture, revocation trails, and role audits enhance legal compliance and patient rights.
- **Compliance engine:** Policy enforcement and automated reporting align with regulators and accreditation bodies.
- **Tamper evidence:** Immutable logs deter fraud and strengthen institutional accountability.

---

## SDG 17 — Partnerships for the goals

- **Insurer & bank integrations:** Claims processing, settlements, and financing support multi‑stakeholder collaboration.
- **Public health data sharing:** Aggregated, privacy‑preserving reporting for ministries and research partners.
- **Standards alignment:** Interoperability profiles enable cross‑border cooperation and scale.

---

## SDG 10 — Reduced inequalities

- **Inclusive onboarding:** Low‑friction digital identity and remote access reduce geographic and income barriers.
- **Subsidy & benefit routing:** Verified eligibility flows support equitable care and financial assistance.
- **Language & accessibility:** Multichannel communication and inclusive UX improve reach.

---

## SDG 11 — Sustainable cities and communities

- **Emergency coordination:** Secure dispatch, triage, and capacity signals support citywide response.
- **Environmental health links:** Data feeds for air/water quality inform preventive care and alerts.
- **Community health programs:** Verified outreach and outcomes tracking improve local services.

---

## SDG 8 — Decent work and economic growth

- **Workforce credentials:** Verified licensing, training, and shift logs protect patients and staff.
- **Operational efficiency:** Automated claims, procurement, and inventory reduce waste and cost.
- **Fair compensation rails:** Transparent payroll and benefits improve retention and morale.

---

## SDG 12 — Responsible consumption and production

- **Supply chain tracking:** Lot/batch tracking for medicines and devices reduces counterfeits and waste.
- **Pharmacy controls:** Dispense audits and interaction checks enhance safety and stewardship.
- **Materials reporting:** Sustainability metrics for procurement support responsible sourcing.

---

## Measurement & audit

- **Impact KPIs:** Access equity, readmission rates, claims cycle time, stockout frequency, and audit findings.
- **Data quality:** Completeness, timeliness, and accuracy checks with automated alerts.
- **Independent review:** Exportable reports and verifier roles for regulators and partners.
